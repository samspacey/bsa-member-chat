# Darlington Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Darlington Building Society
- **Founded:** 1856
- **Headquarters:** Sentinel House, Morton Road, Darlington, County Durham, DL1 4PT
- **Total assets:** ~£923 million (2023/2024)
- **Members:** ~88,760
- **Staff:** ~186 (2022), ~154 (2025 estimate)
- **CEO:** Andrew Craddock (appointed December 2018; previously Chief Executive of Buckinghamshire Building Society)
- **Chair:** Rachel Court (appointed April 2025; previously Chair of Leek Building Society for nine years)
- **CFO:** Steven Forth (appointed October 2022)
- **Deputy CEO:** Christopher Hunter
- **Profit before tax (2023):** £3.8 million
- **Mortgage balances:** ~£770 million (2023)
- **Retail deposits:** ~£793 million (2023)
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its members; profits reinvested into the Society and communities
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes (Registration Number 205895)
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** Helping families with big milestones; a mutual at heart
- **Identity:** A mid-sized, regional building society deeply rooted in the North East of England, known for strong community commitment and its 5% Pledge charitable giving programme

## Products

### Savings

Darlington Building Society offers a comprehensive range of savings accounts designed for different savings goals and life stages:

**Account types:**
- **Easy Access Savings Accounts** — Flexible deposits and withdrawals
- **Regular Savings Accounts** — Encouraging consistent monthly saving habits
- **Children's Savings Accounts** — Teaching children and grandchildren the value of saving
- **ISA (Individual Savings Account)** — Tax-free savings
- **Notice Savings Accounts** — Higher rates in exchange for giving notice before withdrawal
- **Fixed Rate Bonds** — Guaranteed rate for a set term
- **Matured Accounts** — Options for maturing bond and ISA holders

The Society positions savings strongly around family milestones — helping grandparents save for grandchildren, commemorating special occasions, and gifting.

### Mortgages

Darlington Building Society offers a range of mortgage products for various circumstances:

**Mortgage types:**
- **First Time Buyer Mortgages** — Helping first-time buyers onto the property ladder
- **Moving Home Mortgages** — For those purchasing their next home
- **Remortgaging** — Switching mortgage to Darlington from another lender
- **Switching Mortgage Deal** — Existing customers switching to a new deal
- **New Build Mortgages** — For newly constructed properties
- **Other Mortgage Types** — Specialist and niche lending

**Key features:**
- Personal mortgage advisors available by phone and in branch
- Available Monday to Friday 9:00am–6:00pm (Tuesday from 9:30am), Saturday 9:00am–1:00pm
- Intermediary channel for brokers
- Financial support team for members struggling with payments

## Digital & Technology

- **Darlingtonline** — The Society's online banking platform for managing savings accounts and viewing mortgage balances securely. Available on all smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers. Features include account management and a built-in messaging service for account queries.
- **8x8 XCaaS** — In 2021, Darlington deployed 8x8's cloud communications platform as part of wider digital transformation, modernising employee communications and customer engagement
- **eBroker System** — Digital mortgage application processing system for intermediaries
- **Cloud Migration** — The Society has moved its IT infrastructure to the cloud, working with CSI Ltd for flexibility and efficiency
- **Named Building Society of the Year 2021** — Partly due to heavy investment in digital banking, working with UK-based IT company IeDigital
- **Best Companies 2024** — Recognised as an employer

## Community & Values

Darlington Building Society is known for its exceptionally strong community commitment:

### 5% Pledge
- The Society **donates at least 5% of its annual profits** to local charities and good causes
- Managed through the **County Durham Community Foundation**
- In 2022, the Society committed a record **£172,500** to community causes
- By October 2025, the **5% Pledge reached a cumulative £1 million** in donations
- Supports causes relating to **mental health, climate change, poverty, loneliness, and skills development for the unemployed**
- The fund supports charities and voluntary organisations to continue their work, develop services, and fund projects in local communities

### Other Community Involvement
- Active supporter of local charities through the 5% Pledge fund
- CEO Andrew Craddock is a member of the **Darlington Cares** board
- Deputy CEO Chris Hunter is Treasurer at the **700 Club** housing charity in Darlington
- Staff engagement in community activities and volunteer work
- Strong ties to the North East community through its branch network

## Key Differentiators

1. **5% Pledge** — Donating at least 5% of profits to local good causes is a distinctive and generous commitment, reaching £1 million cumulative by 2025
2. **Strong regional presence** — 9 branches across the North East, County Durham, and North Yorkshire provide genuine local accessibility
3. **Award-winning digital transformation** — Named Building Society of the Year 2021 for digital investment
4. **Family-focused savings** — Positioning around helping families with milestones, intergenerational saving
5. **Experienced leadership** — CEO Andrew Craddock has 39+ years in retail financial services; strong board with deep sector experience
6. **Mutual ethos** — Member-owned, profits reinvested for members' benefit
7. **Mid-sized with scale** — At ~£923 million in assets, large enough to offer competitive products while remaining community-focused
8. **Investment in people** — Best Companies 2024 recognition demonstrates commitment to colleague welfare

## Strengths

- Exceptional community giving through the 5% Pledge (5% of profits, not a token gesture)
- Good geographical coverage across the North East with 9 branches
- Strong digital investment and modernisation
- Experienced and stable leadership team
- Competitive savings and mortgage products
- High customer satisfaction (rated 4.9/5 on Smart Money People with over 9,100 reviews as of 2024)

## Weaknesses

- Geographically concentrated in North East England — limited reach outside the region
- Some Trustpilot reviews mention customer service frustrations
- Mid-sized society competing against both larger societies with greater scale and smaller ones with more niche focus
- Branch opening hours vary significantly (Middlesbrough only 4 days a week)

## Member Demographics

- Primarily serves communities across **North East England, County Durham, and North Yorkshire**
- Mix of savers and mortgage borrowers from the Tees Valley, North Yorkshire, and surrounding areas
- Strong family demographic — society actively targets intergenerational saving (grandparents saving for grandchildren)
- Likely skews towards local, community-minded members who value personal service and branch access
- ~88,760 members as of 2024
