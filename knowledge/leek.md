# Leek Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Leek United Building Society (trading as Leek Building Society)
- **Founded:** 1 January 1863 (as the Leek United Permanent Benefit Building Society)
- **Headquarters:** 50 St. Edward Street, Leek, Staffordshire, ST13 5DL
- **Total assets:** £1,313.9 million (December 2023, up 6.1% on 2022)
- **Ranking:** 17th largest building society in the UK
- **Staff:** 182 (2023)
- **CEO:** Andy Deeks (appointed June 2024; succeeded Andrew Healy)
- **Mutual status:** Independent mutual building society owned by its members
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes (Registration Number 100014)
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Motto:** "Firm and Lasting (as the pyramids)"
- **Tagline / positioning:** "A force for good" — helping communities grow so they can do great things today and even better things tomorrow
- **Identity:** A mid-to-large building society with 160+ years of heritage, known for specialist mortgage lending, community focus, and award-winning service. Based in the Staffordshire Moorlands with a strong regional branch network.
- **Smart Money People rating:** 4.90 out of 5 (excellent)
- **Award:** UK's Best Specialist Mortgage Provider at the 2025 British Bank Awards

## Products

### Savings

Leek Building Society offers a broad range of savings accounts:

**Account types:**
- **Easy Access** — Flexible deposits and withdrawals
- **ISAs (Individual Savings Accounts)** — Tax-free savings options
- **Children's Accounts** — Savings for under-18s
- **Regular Saver** — For consistent monthly saving
- **Fixed Rate Bonds** — Guaranteed rate for a set term
- **Notice Accounts** — Higher rates with notice period

**Key features:**
- Compare savings accounts tool on website
- Online, phone, and in-branch account opening
- Savings FAQ and guidance resources
- Confirmation of Payee supported
- UK Savings Week participation

## Strengths

- Award-winning specialist mortgage expertise (2025 British Bank Awards)
- Exceptional customer satisfaction (4.90/5 Smart Money People)
- Substantial asset base (£1.3bn) providing financial stability
- Good branch network across three counties
- Charitable Foundation with structured community giving
- Environmental and diversity commitments (Green Ambition, Women in Finance)
- Broader product range including insurance and financial planning
- Successfully resisted hostile takeover bid in 1999 (maintaining independence)

## Weaknesses

- Geographically concentrated in Staffordshire, Cheshire, and Derbyshire
- New CEO (appointed June 2024) — transition period
- Branch opening hours limited (some branches only open until 1pm or 2pm)
- Specialist mortgage positioning may not attract straightforward borrowers seeking best rates
- Digital capabilities appear functional but not cutting-edge

## Member Demographics

- Primarily serves **Staffordshire, Cheshire, and Derbyshire** communities
- Mix of local savers using branches and mortgage borrowers (many via intermediaries)
- Specialist mortgage borrowers who may be nationally distributed
- Market town residents who value local branch access and personal service
- Members who value independence and mutual ownership (demonstrated by rejecting the 1999 takeover bid)
- ~182 employees suggests a relatively lean operation for its asset size
