- **CEO:** Simon Beresford (Chief Executive)
- **Chair:** Julie Nicholson
- **Profit before tax (2023):** £1.15 million
- **New mortgage lending (2023):** £72 million
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its saving and borrowing members
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA regulated:** Yes (Register no. 156580)
- **FSCS protected:** Yes (up to £120,000)
- **Tagline / positioning:** "Opening doors for teachers. Since 1966"
- **Identity:** The UK's only building society dedicated to helping teachers and education professionals onto the property ladder

Teachers Building Society operates as a **branchless society** — it has no public-facing branch offices. All business is conducted:

## Products

### Savings

Savings with Teachers Building Society directly support the society's mortgage lending to teachers — a key part of the mutual model.

**Savings for teachers/education workers:**
- Teacher-exclusive savings accounts with preferential terms
- Accounts designed specifically for those working in education

**Savings for everyone (open to all professions):**
- **Easy Access Savings** — Variable rate, instant access
- **Cash ISAs** — Tax-free savings
- **Notice Savings** — Higher rates for giving advance notice of withdrawals
- **Fixed Rate Savings** — Locked-in rates for set terms

**Business & Organisational Savings:**
- Corporate savings accounts
- Charitable organisation savings
- SIPP & SSAS (pension) savings

### Mortgages

Mortgages are the heart of Teachers Building Society's purpose. They were founded specifically to lend to teachers when mainstream lenders wouldn't.

**Key lending features:**
- Lending up to **7 times joint income** for teachers/education professionals (increased from 5x in 2025)
- Up to 95% LTV for teacher first-time buyers
- Available for properties in England and Wales
- Individual case assessment with understanding of teacher career patterns

**Teacher mortgage products:**
- **First Time Buyer Mortgages** — Core product for teachers with smaller deposits
- **Early Career Teacher Mortgages** — For NQTs and those starting their teaching careers
- **Supply/Contract Teacher Mortgages** — Understanding of non-standard income patterns
- **Experienced Teacher Mortgages** — For teachers at any career stage
- **Retired Teacher Mortgages** — Options for those who have retired from teaching
- **Shared Ownership Mortgages** — For teachers buying a share of a home

**Non-teacher mortgages (open to all):**
- **Holiday Let Mortgages** — Minimum 25% deposit
- **Buy to Let Mortgages** — Minimum 25% deposit

*Note: Holiday let and BTL mortgages are offered to non-teachers to balance the society's lending book (regulatory requirements around high-LTV lending proportions) and to apply specialist knowledge to the investment property market.*

## Community & Values

- **Core purpose:** Helping every teacher across England and Wales to make home ownership a reality
- **Founded on social justice:** In the 1960s, teachers (especially women) were considered unattractive lending prospects by high street providers. The NUT created the society to change this.
- **100% teacher-first:** Everything the society does comes back to supporting teacher homeownership
- **Colleague volunteering:** Each team member gets up to two working days per year for community/charitable work
- **Community activities include:**
  - Volunteering in education settings
  - Foodbank drives and donations
  - Providing advice on job applications and personal finance through school partnerships
  - Beach cleans for cleaner coastlines
  - Tree planting and woodland regeneration
- **Environmental commitment:** Use of Ecosia as default search engine, energy-efficient lighting, reduced paper usage
- **Sustainability guide:** Published eco-friendly home living tips for teacher members

## Key Differentiators

1. **Only building society specialising in teachers** — Unique positioning in the UK market; no other society has this exclusive focus
2. **Up to 7x income lending** — Significantly higher than typical high street limits, recognising teacher income stability
3. **Understanding of teacher careers** — Accepts NQTs, supply teachers, contract workers, and retirees; understands term-time working patterns
4. **Purpose-driven mutual** — Founded to solve a social problem (teacher homeownership); purpose hasn't changed in nearly 60 years
5. **Savings directly fund teacher mortgages** — Clear mutual link between saving and lending
6. **Small, personal service** — ~50-80 staff means genuine individual attention
7. **Branchless model** — Lower overheads allow competitive rates and focus on core mission
8. **Buyers Club** — Sign-up programme to help prospective teacher buyers prepare for homeownership

## Strengths

- Unique niche positioning with no direct competitors
- Deep understanding of teacher employment patterns and income
- Strong sense of purpose and identity
- High income multiples (up to 7x) for teacher borrowers
- Competitive savings rates that fund teacher lending
- Lean operational model
- Historical connection to the National Education Union

## Weaknesses

- Very small society — limited product range compared to larger competitors
- No branch network — may not suit members who prefer face-to-face service
- Limited geographic lending (England and Wales only)
- Small staff team means capacity constraints
- Low public profile outside the education sector
- Non-teacher mortgage products (BTL/holiday let) limited

## Member Demographics

- **Primary members:** Teachers and education professionals across England and Wales
- **Mortgage borrowers:** Predominantly first-time buyer teachers, NQTs, and early-career educators
- **Savers:** Mix of education professionals and general public (savings open to all)
- **Target audience:** Anyone working in education who wants to buy a home, particularly those with smaller deposits
- **Geographic spread:** National across England and Wales, with Dorset-area local presence
