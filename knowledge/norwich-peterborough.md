# Norwich & Peterborough Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Norwich & Peterborough Building Society (N&P) — now a trading name of Yorkshire Building Society
- **Founded:** 1986 (merger of Norwich Building Society, est. 1852, and Peterborough Building Society, est. 1860)
- **Headquarters:** Originally Lynch Wood, Peterborough; now Bradford, West Yorkshire (YBS Group HQ)
- **Status:** **No longer an independent society.** Merged with Yorkshire Building Society in 2011. The N&P brand was progressively withdrawn from 2017, with all remaining N&P branches closing on 6 July 2018 and reopening as YBS branches on 9 July 2018.
- **Total assets at merger:** ~£4.9 billion (ninth largest UK building society at time of merger)
- **Members at peak:** Over 500,000
- **Staff at peak:** Over 800 (roughly half based at Lynch Wood, Peterborough)
- **BSA member:** Yes (as part of Yorkshire Building Society)
- **FSCS protected:** Yes (under Yorkshire Building Society)

## History

- The Norwich Building Society was founded in 1852 as the Norwich District Provident Permanent Benefit Building and Freehold Land Society
- The Peterborough Building Society was established in 1860 by railway workers at the Corn Exchange, Peterborough, to help members build homes during the housing shortage caused by railway expansion
- The two societies merged in 1986 to form Norwich & Peterborough Building Society
- In 1988, N&P opened a new headquarters at Lynch Wood business park, Peterborough, opened by Queen Elizabeth II
- In 1990, N&P opened a branch in Gibraltar (closed 2014)
- At its peak, N&P had over 45 branch offices, mainly in East Anglia and surrounding counties (Northamptonshire, Lincolnshire), plus Gibraltar and High Holborn, London
- N&P merged with Yorkshire Building Society in 2011
- In January 2017, YBS announced the closure of 28 (later 31) N&P branches, all current accounts, and the eventual withdrawal of the N&P brand
- All remaining N&P branches closed on 6 July 2018 and were rebranded as YBS branches

## Branch Network

- **Current:** No dedicated N&P branches exist. The brand is retained only as an online/legacy trading name of Yorkshire Building Society.
- **Historical:** Over 45 branches across East Anglia, Northamptonshire, Lincolnshire, London, and Gibraltar

## Products

As a trading name of Yorkshire Building Society, N&P no longer offers independent products. Any residual N&P-branded products are managed under YBS.

### Historical Products
- Retail banking (current accounts — now discontinued)
- Savings and investments
- Mortgages
- Surveying services
- General insurance broking
- Share dealing

## Digital & Technology

- All services now managed through Yorkshire Building Society's digital platform
- N&P members transitioned to YBS online systems following the merger

## Community & Values

- N&P had a strong presence in the East Anglia community during its independent years
- The Peterborough headquarters retained an important operational presence for YBS after the merger
- YBS continues to operate former N&P branch locations under the Yorkshire brand

## Key Points for Members

1. **N&P is no longer independent** — it is a trading name of Yorkshire Building Society since 2011
2. The N&P brand was largely withdrawn in 2017-2018
3. Former N&P members are now YBS members with full access to YBS products and services
4. Any queries should be directed to Yorkshire Building Society
5. YBS Group includes Yorkshire Building Society, Chelsea Building Society, and Norwich & Peterborough Building Society as trading names

## Strengths (Historical)

- Strong regional identity in East Anglia
- Extensive branch network across eastern England
- Full-service offering including current accounts, savings, mortgages, insurance and share dealing
- Heritage dating back to 1852

## Weaknesses / Challenges

- Brand effectively discontinued — no independent identity remaining
- Branch network entirely absorbed into YBS
- Members lost the local, regional character of their society
- Current account facilities were withdrawn as part of the brand wind-down
