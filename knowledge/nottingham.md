# Nottingham Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Nottingham Building Society (formerly Nottingham Permanent Benefit Building Society)
- **Founded:** 1849, by a group led by Samuel Fox (1781-1868), a Quaker and prominent local grocer
- **Headquarters:** Nottingham House, 3 Fulforth Street, Nottingham, NG1 3DL
- **Total assets:** £5,226.7 million / £5.2 billion (December 2024)
- **Revenue:** £88.4 million (2024)
- **Operating income:** £24.1 million (2024)
- **Net income:** £13.9 million (2024)
- **Total equity:** £253.3 million (2024)
- **Staff:** 478 (2024)
- **CEO:** Sue Hayes
- **Chairman:** Robin Ashton (previously Chair of Leeds Building Society for 7+ years)
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its members
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes (Registration Number 200785)
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** "Being smart with money is what we do" — helping people own homes, keep savings safe, and manage money for over 175 years
- **Identity:** One of the UK's largest building societies, with significant scale (£5.2bn assets, 478 staff, 31 branches), deep Nottinghamshire roots dating to 1849, and a comprehensive product range. Known as "The Nottingham."
- **Customer satisfaction:** 94.1% of customers rated service as good or excellent (based on 4,971 surveys, Jan-Nov 2025); 90.8% said they would use services again (based on 2,748 surveys)
- **Website:** www.thenottingham.com

## Branch Network

Nottingham Building Society operates **31 branches** — one of the largest branch networks among UK building societies:

Branches are primarily located across **Nottinghamshire** and the surrounding East Midlands region. The large network demonstrates a strong commitment to maintaining a physical high street presence.

## Products

### Savings

Nottingham Building Society offers a comprehensive savings range:

**Account types:**
- **Instant Access** — Flexible deposits and withdrawals
- **Limited Access** — Restricted withdrawal accounts with higher rates
- **Individual Savings Accounts (ISAs)** — Tax-free savings
- **Fixed Rate Bonds** — Guaranteed rates for set terms
- **Regular Savings** — For consistent monthly saving
- **eSavings** — Online-only savings products
- **Children's Savings** — Accounts for young savers

### Mortgages

Nottingham Building Society provides mortgages through a partnership model:

**Key features:**
- Mortgages offered through **Mortgage Advice Bureau** — Nottingham BS is an introducer
- Mortgage Advice Bureau is a trading name of Brook Financial Services Limited (FRN 529047)
- There may be a fee for mortgage advice (up to 1.00%, typical fee 0.30% of amount borrowed)
- Available for a wide range of mortgage types
- Branch-based mortgage advice

### Insurance

- Insurance products available to members
- Protection products offered through the Mortgage Advice Bureau relationship

## Strengths

- Major scale (£5.2bn assets) provides competitive positioning and financial strength
- Largest branch network (31 branches) among mid-tier societies
- Excellent customer satisfaction (94.1% good/excellent)
- Strong revenue and profitability (£88.4m revenue, £13.9m net income)
- Over 175 years of heritage and trust
- Comprehensive product range across savings, mortgages, and insurance
- Professional mortgage advice through Mortgage Advice Bureau partnership
- Women in Finance Charter commitment
- Strong governance with experienced chairman

## Weaknesses

- Geographically concentrated in Nottinghamshire and East Midlands
- Mortgage advice bureau model means less direct control over mortgage customer experience
- Mortgage advice fees (up to 1% though typical 0.30%) may deter some borrowers
- Large branch network brings higher operating costs
- Competition from other large Midlands-based societies (Coventry, Skipton, Yorkshire)
- Less well-known nationally than the top-5 building societies despite significant size
