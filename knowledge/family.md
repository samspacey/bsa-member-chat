# Family Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** National Counties Building Society (trading as Family Building Society)
- **Founded:** 1896 (National Counties Building Society); Family Building Society brand launched 14 July 2014
- **Headquarters:** Ebbisham House, 30 Church Street, Epsom, Surrey, KT17 4NL
- **Total assets:** ~£2.2 billion (making it the UK's 11th largest building society)
- **Members:** Over 69,000
- **Staff:** ~172 (2019)
- **CEO:** Mark Bogard
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its members; Family Building Society members are members of National Counties Building Society and vice versa
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes (for savings; note the Family Mortgage linked savings product has specific FSCS limitations)
- **Tagline / positioning:** Innovative and flexible mortgage and savings solutions for modern families
- **Identity:** A specialist building society focused on helping families across generations, particularly parents helping children buy property. Positions itself as the first new building society brand since Ecology BS in 1981, though it operates as a trading name of the established National Counties BS.

## Branch Network

Family Building Society has a very small physical footprint:

1. **Epsom Branch** — 30 Church Street, Epsom, Surrey (also the head office location)

This is essentially an **online and telephone-based** building society. Transactions can be carried out by phone, post, internet, and at the single Epsom branch. The business model is primarily intermediary-led for mortgages and direct/online for savings.

## Products

### Savings

Family Building Society offers a range of savings accounts with competitive rates, all manageable online, by post, or at the Epsom branch:

**Account types:**
- **Easy Access Savings** — Flexible access with variable interest rates
- **Notice Accounts** — Higher rates with notice period for withdrawals
- **Fixed Rate Bonds** — Guaranteed rates for fixed terms (1, 2, 3, 5 year options available)
- **Cash ISAs** — Tax-free savings with easy access, notice, and fixed rate options
- **Market Tracker Accounts** — Linked to Bank of England base rate, providing transparency on rate movements (e.g., Market Tracker Cash ISA, Market Tracker Saver)

**Key savings features:**
- Tiered rates on some products
- Monthly interest option available on some bonds
- Minimum opening amounts typically from £1 to £1,000 depending on account type
- Award-winning service

### Mortgages

Family Building Society is particularly known for its **specialist and flexible mortgage products** designed to help families in non-standard situations:

**Mortgage product range:**
- **Standard Residential Mortgages** — Fixed, variable, and tracker options for moving home and remortgaging
- **First Time Buyer / Family Assisted Mortgages** — The flagship "Family Mortgage" where family members deposit savings as security to help loved ones get on the property ladder
- **Later Life Lending** — Mortgages for borrowers over 65, retirement interest-only, and equity release (via partner Key Retirement Solutions)
- **Expat Mortgages** — For UK nationals living abroad who want to buy property in the UK
- **Buy to Let** — For individual landlords and limited companies
- **Self-Employed Mortgages** — Tailored for self-employed borrowers

**Key mortgage features:**
- **No age discrimination** — "Over 65? No problem. We don't judge based on age"
- **Tailored credit checks** rather than automated credit scoring
- **Real human underwriters** with years of experience who "listen to your story"
- **Personal and common sense approach** to lending
- Payment waiver product offered in conjunction with CUNA Mutual Group (mortgage payments met for up to 6 months if borrower loses job through no fault of their own)
- Mortgage insurance products from Genworth Financial for high LTV lending
- Financial advice available through appointed advisers (Charles Derby)
- Equity release through Key Retirement Solutions

### Research & Thought Leadership

Family Building Society has invested in research partnerships:
- **LSE Report (2019):** "The Bank of Mum and Dad: How it really works" — research on parental financial support for property purchase
- **LSE Report (2018):** "A tax too far? Monitoring the impact of SDLT" — research on stamp duty's impact on the housing market

## Digital & Technology

- **Online account management** — Savings accounts can be opened and managed online
- **Affordability calculators** — Online mortgage affordability calculator and buy-to-let calculator
- **Primarily digital and telephone-based service model** — The society's single-branch model means digital and phone channels are the primary interaction points for most members
- **Intermediary portal** — Online platform for mortgage brokers

## Community & Values

- **Mutual ethos:** Member-owned, profits reinvested for members' benefit rather than paid to shareholders
- **Family focus:** Core purpose is enabling families to help each other across generations
- **Regular member engagement:** Frequently asks members for views on performance and improvements
- **Award-winning service:** Recognised for hard work and innovation

## Key Differentiators

1. **Family-focused proposition** — Entire brand built around intergenerational family financial support, particularly the "Bank of Mum and Dad" concept
2. **Later life lending expertise** — No age-based discrimination; strong offering for over-65s and retirees
3. **Expat mortgage specialism** — Serves UK nationals overseas who want UK property
4. **Human underwriting** — Real people making lending decisions based on individual circumstances, not algorithms
5. **Research-led brand** — LSE partnerships give credibility and insight into family financial dynamics
6. **Scale with specialism** — £2.2bn in assets (11th largest BS) provides financial strength while maintaining niche focus
7. **Payment waiver innovation** — First UK building society to include a job-loss payment waiver product
8. **Tracker savings products** — Market Tracker accounts linked to Bank of England base rate provide transparency

## Strengths

- Strong specialist positioning in family-assisted and later-life lending
- Substantial asset base (~£2.2bn) for a specialist society
- Human, individual underwriting approach
- Expat mortgage capability (relatively rare among building societies)
- Research partnerships with LSE lend credibility
- Award-winning service and innovative products
- Over 69,000 members and growing

## Weaknesses

- Only one physical branch (Epsom) — no local presence for most members
- Family Mortgage linked savings don't have full FSCS protection
- Mortgage rates may not be the most competitive for straightforward cases (designed for specialist situations)
- Brand awareness potentially lower than long-established societies — many may not realise it's a trading name of National Counties BS
- Limited geographical community involvement compared to societies with branch networks

## Member Demographics

- **Geographically dispersed** — Members across the UK (and expats overseas) rather than concentrated in one region
- **Family-oriented** — Parents and grandparents helping children/grandchildren buy property
- **Later life borrowers** — Over-65s and retirees seeking mortgage products
- **Expats** — UK nationals living abroad
- **Landlords** — Both UK-based and overseas buy-to-let investors
- **Self-employed** — Borrowers who struggle with high street lender criteria
- **Modern families** — The "modern family" positioning suggests diverse family structures
- Over 69,000 members
