# Market Harborough Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Market Harborough Building Society
- **Founded:** 1870 (established at The Square in Market Harborough)
- **Headquarters:** Newcombe House, 16 The Point, Market Harborough, Leicestershire LE16 7QU (newly refurbished offices opened March 2024)
- **Total assets:** £722 million (2023)
- **Mortgage book growth:** 20% for two consecutive years (2022-2023); 45% over three years
- **New mortgage lending (2023):** £198 million (record level)
- **Savings inflows (2023):** £129 million (record, up from £92m in 2022)
- **Staff:** ~128
- **CEO:** Iain Kirkpatrick (Chief Executive)
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its members since 1870
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** "Your award-winning specialist lender" — complex case and bridging finance expert
- **Identity:** One of the UK's fastest-growing building societies; specialist in complex mortgages and bridging finance, operating primarily through intermediaries

## Products

### Savings

Market Harborough was awarded **Best Building Society Savings Provider** by MoneyComms in their Top Performers list for 2024, based on product features, transparency, simplicity, consistency and website navigation.

**Account types:**
- **Fixed Term Bond Accounts** — Fixed rate savings for set terms
- **Easy Access Accounts** — Variable rate, instant access savings
- **Notice Accounts** — From 30 days upwards; flexible approach
- **ISA Accounts** — Tax-free savings
- **Organisation Easy Access Accounts** — For businesses and organisations
- **Organisation Notice Accounts** — From 60 days upwards for organisations

**Online service** available for account management.

### Mortgages (Specialist/Complex Lending)

Market Harborough Building Society has repositioned itself as a **specialist lender of choice** for complex mortgage cases and bridging finance, operating primarily through mortgage intermediaries (brokers).

**Key mortgage features:**
- Lending between **£200,000 and £5 million**
- Up to **80% LTV** on properties in England, Wales, and central Scotland (mainland)
- Scotland postcodes accepted: EH, FK, G, KA, KY, ML, PA, TD
- Daily Credit Committee led by CEO for fast, connected decision-making
- **24 hours' notice guaranteed** before any product changes (Best for Brokers Promise)
- Feefo **Platinum Trusted Service Award** for three consecutive years (to 2026)
- Feefo **Exceptional Service Award** (awarded to only 30 providers)

**Mortgage product range:**
- Residential mortgages for complex cases
- **Bridging finance** — A key specialism
- Unusual/non-standard property mortgages
- Non-standard income mortgages (self-employed, complex income structures)
- Flexible, award-winning specialist products

**Best for Brokers Promise:**
- Specialist BDMs (Business Development Managers) assigned by region
- Open and honest two-way communication
- Long-term broker relationships (usually help with several cases per year)
- Proactive approach to finding ways to say "yes" to complex cases

## Key Differentiators

1. **Complex case specialist** — Positioned as go-to lender for unusual, difficult mortgage cases that mainstream lenders decline
2. **Bridging finance expertise** — Specialist product line for short-term property finance
3. **One of the UK's fastest-growing building societies** — 45% mortgage book growth in three years; record lending and savings inflows
4. **Daily CEO-led Credit Committee** — Rapid, connected decision-making on mortgage cases
5. **Best for Brokers Promise** — Formal commitment to intermediary service standards including 24-hour product change notice
6. **High lending limits** — Up to £5 million per case with 80% LTV
7. **Award-winning service** — Feefo Platinum and Exceptional Service awards for consistent broker satisfaction
8. **New state-of-the-art headquarters** — Modern working environment supporting growth

## Strengths

- Exceptional growth trajectory — one of the UK's fastest-growing societies
- Strong specialist/complex case niche with limited competition
- Award-winning broker service (Feefo Platinum three years running)
- Daily Credit Committee enables fast, flexible decision-making
- New headquarters supports continued growth
- Competitive savings products (MoneyComms award winner)
- Strong presence in Leicestershire and Northamptonshire communities

## Weaknesses

- Intermediary-only mortgage distribution — no direct mortgage advice for most borrowers
- Minimum lending of £200,000 excludes lower-value properties
- Limited branch network — only 5 branches, all in a small geographic area
- Many branches operate reduced hours (mornings only, limited days)
- Recent branch closure (Corby, May 2025) suggests potential further rationalisation
- Rapid growth requires careful risk management
- Scotland lending limited to central mainland postcodes only

## Member Demographics

- **Mortgage borrowers:** Complex case borrowers across England, Wales, and central Scotland; often self-employed, non-standard income, unusual properties; referred by mortgage brokers
- **Savers:** Local members in Leicestershire/Northamptonshire who use branches, plus nationwide online/postal savers
- **Broker partners:** Key stakeholder group — specialist brokers dealing regularly with complex cases
- **Geographic split:** Savings concentrated locally; mortgage lending is national
- **Lending range:** £200k-£5m suggests predominantly mid-to-high value property borrowers

What I encountered instead was a masterclass in how not to handle customers who are trying to stay ahead of the curve.

The initial silence was telling. Weeks passed with no acknowledgement, no holding reply, no sign that anyone was paying attention. When a reply finally arrived, it read like a template churned out after reluctantly dusting off the inbox: references to missing emails, apologies that rang hollow, and an invitation to resend everything as though the burden naturally sits with the customer rather than the institution.

The substance that followed was even more disheartening. Rather than engaging with the perfectly reasonable idea that a short, well-defined period might warrant proportionate, preventative support, the focus fixed instantly on “affordability assessments”, “financial difficulty”, and the need for detailed income-and-expenditure forms. In regulatory terms, what troubles me most is how this experience sits against the FCA’s own expectations of lenders. The FCA’s mortgage support guidance and Dear CEO communications on the cost of living make it clear that firms should offer prospective and tailored forbearance where customers indicate they are, or reasonably expect to be, in payment difficulty - including BEFORE any payment is actually missed – and that support should be appropriate to the customer’s individual circumstances rather than driven by rigid process. Layered on top of that, the Consumer Duty now requires firms to act to deliver good outcomes, avoid foreseeable harm and support customers in pursuing their financial objectives, not simply protect their own risk metrics. Against that backdrop, the slow response, the lack of meaningful engagement, and the default push into a full “hardship” framing with credit-file warnings - rather than a proportionate, constructive conversation about short-term flexibility – feels, in my view, out of step with both the letter and the spirit of what the FCA says good mortgage lenders should be doing.

A society that prides itself on doing things “the right way” should not make customers feel like an inconvenience for trying to engage early and responsibly. And it should certainly not treat every enquiry as though it must be funnelled into the same bureaucracy that applies to long-term, distressed borrowers.

In short, this experience left me with the view that MHBS struggles to live up to the values it promotes. If you are looking for a lender that genuinely listens, applies proportionality, and engages constructively with members who act in good faith, you may find the gulf between their marketing and their behaviour uncomfortably wide."
