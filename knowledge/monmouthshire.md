# Monmouthshire Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview
- Founded: 1869
- Headquarters: John Frost Square, Newport, South Wales
- Assets: ~£600 million
- Members: ~25,000
- Staff: ~80
- Mutual building society (member-owned, not shareholder-owned)

## Branch Network
- **Newport (Head Office)**: John Frost Square, Newport NP20 1PX - Main branch, open Mon-Fri 9-5, Sat 9-12
- **Abergavenny**: Cross Street - Smaller branch
- **Chepstow**: High Street - Agency arrangement

## Products
### Savings
- Regular Saver: Up to 5% on monthly deposits (max £300/month)
- Cash ISA: Competitive rates for tax-free savings
- Easy Access: Instant access savings
- Fixed Rate Bonds: 1, 2, 3 year terms
- Junior ISA: For under-18s

### Mortgages
- First-time buyer mortgages
- Home mover mortgages
- Remortgages
- Buy-to-let (limited)
- Shared ownership
- Help to Buy Wales support

## Recent News & Changes
- 2025: Launched new mobile app (basic functionality)
- 2024: Upgraded online banking platform
- 2023: Celebrated 155th anniversary with community grants
- Ongoing: Digital transformation programme

## Customer Sentiment (from reviews)
### Positive Themes
- Friendly, personal service
- Staff know customers by name
- Good mortgage rates for local properties
- Community involvement
- Welsh/local identity

### Negative Themes
- App is basic compared to big banks
- Phone wait times can be long
- Limited opening hours
- Website feels dated
- Some processes still require paper/branch visits

## Competitors in the Area
- Principality Building Society (larger, more digital)
- Nationwide
- High street banks (Barclays, Lloyds, NatWest)
- Online challengers (Monzo, Starling, Chase)

## Strategic Priorities (publicly stated)
- Maintaining personal service while improving digital
- Supporting local communities
- Helping first-time buyers in Wales
- Sustainable growth while remaining mutual

## Common Customer Questions
1. "Are you going to close branches?"
2. "When will the app have more features?"
3. "Can I do X online or do I have to come in?"
4. "What makes you different from the big banks?"
5. "Do you offer mortgages for properties outside Wales?"

## Local Context
- Newport is undergoing regeneration
- Housing affordability is a major issue in South Wales
- Strong community identity in the valleys
- Many customers have multi-generational relationships with the society
- Welsh language services available
