# Newcastle Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Newcastle Building Society
- **Founded:** 1863 (Newcastle Permanent Building Society); current form created 1980 through merger of Grainger (founded 1861) and Newcastle Permanent Building Societies
- **Headquarters:** Newcastle upon Tyne, England
- **Total assets:** £5,312.5 million (2022); continued growth through 2024
- **Revenue:** £119.7 million (2022)
- **Operating income:** £31.7 million (2022)
- **Operating profit (2024):** £34.2 million (before impairments and provisions; up 9% on 2023)
- **Profit before tax (2024):** £15.7 million (reduced from £29.1m due to voluntary support for Philips Trust customers)
- **Gross mortgage lending (2024):** £1.2 billion (record, exceeding 2023's £1.1bn record)
- **Members:** Significant membership base (specific number not publicly stated)
- **Staff:** ~1,393 employees (2022)
- **CEO:** Andrew Haigh
- **Chair:** James Ramsbotham CBE DL
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its saving and borrowing members
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Customer satisfaction:** 96% (2024); NPS +86
- **Tagline / positioning:** "Connecting our communities with a better financial future"
- **Rank:** 7th largest building society in the UK (previously described as 8th; grown through organic growth and mergers)
- **Identity:** Large, regional mutual deeply committed to North East England, Cumbria, and North Yorkshire; strong branch presence; financial advice in every branch; community-focused purpose-driven organisation

## Branch Network

Newcastle Building Society has **32 branches** (as of 2024) across the North East, Cumbria, and North Yorkshire:

- Branches stretch across the Society's heartland regions
- **Pickering** — 32nd branch, opened 2024, restoring access to financial services in the town
- **North Shields** — Relocated to share space with YMCA North Tyneside community hub
- **Hartlepool** — Full branch refurbishment in 2024
- **Middlesbrough** — Relocated to new facility in 2024
- **Newcastle city centre** — New flagship branch in development
- **Cobalt Business Park** — Office opened 2008 in North Tyneside

**Branch investment:** ~£10m invested since 2015 in new branch locations and refurbishments. Multi-bank kiosks through fintech partner OneBanx within some branches.

**Manchester Building Society brand:** Following 2023 merger with Manchester Building Society (completed 1 July 2023), plans announced to breathe new life into the Manchester brand with new branches, starting in Manchester city centre. The North West is one of the four worst-hit regions for bank/building society closures.

## Products

### Savings

Newcastle Building Society offers comprehensive savings products:

- **Branch-based savings** — Strong focus on branch savings; branch savings balances grew by £442.2m in 2024 (14.9% growth vs market average of 5.4% across all channels)
- **Range includes:** Instant access, notice accounts, fixed rate bonds, Cash ISAs, children's accounts
- **Average savings rates** were 0.56% higher than market average in 2024, resulting in £28.1m more interest for members

**Key features:**
- Consistent above-market savings rates
- Branch savings growth significantly outperforming market
- Combination of branch and digital channels

### Mortgages

Newcastle Building Society is a major mortgage lender:

- **Residential mortgages** — Full range for purchase and remortgage
- **Gross lending of £1.2bn** in 2024 (record)
- **SVR of 6.94%** throughout 2024 — one of the most competitive on the market (vs 7.87% market average), saving SVR borrowers ~£2.8m in interest
- Available through branches and intermediaries

### Financial Advice

A key differentiator — **financial advice available in every branch:**

- **Newcastle Financial Advisers Limited** — Wholly-owned subsidiary
- Provides advice on investments, pensions, life and protection insurance, inheritance tax planning
- Achieved VouchedFor 'Top Rated Firm' status three consecutive years (average 4.9/5)
- Advice available to every customer, "not solely a service for the wealthy"
- Appointed representative of The Openwork Partnership

### Subsidiary Companies

- **Newcastle Financial Advisers** — Financial advice
- **Manchester Building Society** — Trading name (merged July 2023)
- **Newcastle Strategic Solutions** — Savings management platform

## Community & Values

- **Newcastle Building Society Community Fund** — At the Community Foundation; endowment of ~£3.5m
  - £1.1m donation in 2024; £140,000 in grants distributed reaching 200,000+ people through 24 regional charities
  - Priorities: food poverty, homelessness, debt management, employability, sustainability
- **Newcastle United Foundation partnership** — 200+ financial coaching sessions/scam awareness talks across 50 schools reaching ~5,000 pupils; 33 employability sessions benefiting 5,000+ participants with 76 colleague volunteers
- **Colleague volunteering:** Over 10,000 hours (~1,400 days) of volunteering in 2024
- **Philips Trust support:** Voluntary financial support offered to customers impacted by the Philips Trust collapse (reduced 2024 profits)
- **Branch commitment:** "We see a future for branches and the role they can play within a community"
- **Real Living Wage:** Committed employer
- **Diversity and inclusion:** Building connections and behaviours to encourage D&I
- **Environmental sustainability:** Caring for environment, ensuring sustainability for future generations
