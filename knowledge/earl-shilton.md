# Earl Shilton Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Earl Shilton Building Society (trading as "esbs")
- **Founded:** 3 January 1857 (first meeting held 17 December 1856 at the Royal Oak Club Room, High Street, Earl Shilton; first subscriptions paid 3 January 1857)
- **Headquarters:** 22 The Hollow, Earl Shilton, Leicestershire LE9 7NB (at The Hollow since 1940; extensively refurbished 2013)
- **Total assets:** £183 million (March 2025) — second-smallest building society in the UK by total assets
- **Members:** 13,358 (March 2025); over 15,000 referenced on website
- **Staff:** 40 (March 2025)
- **CEO:** Scott Devereux (Chief Executive)
- **Deputy Chief Executive & Finance Director:** Stephen Wigfull
- **Chair:** Alex Robinson
- **Mutual status:** Small mutual organisation, owned by its members
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** "as individual as you"
- **Identity:** Very small, community-rooted building society in Leicestershire; emphasis on personal, individual service and treating each member as a person

## Products

### Savings

Earl Shilton offers a comprehensive range of savings accounts for UK residents, all with passbooks and available via branches and post.

**Regular Savings:**
- **Monthly Saver** — For steady savings growth; competitive rates; deposits between £1-£500 per month (can deposit more than once a month within limit)

**Tax-Free Savings:**
- **Cash ISAs** — Range of ISA options
- **90 Days Notice Cash ISA** — Variable rate with low minimum balance
- **Junior Cash ISA** — Tax-free savings for children's future

**Savings for Young People:**
- **Child Trust Fund Account** — Managing government scheme funds with additional savings, tax-free until age 18
- **Young Saver Account** — For ages 0-18; competitive rates; hands-on approach to money management

**Age-specific accounts:**
- **Heritage Account** — Bonus account for over-50s; limited instant access with conditional bonus
- **Progress Account** — Bonus account for under-50s; limited instant access with conditional bonus

**Key savings features:**
- All accounts come with a passbook
- Operated from branches and through the post
- Simple, straightforward products — a core principle
- FSCS protected

### Mortgages

Earl Shilton Building Society specialises in personal, case-by-case mortgage assessment — "however unique."

**Current status:**
- **Temporarily paused direct mortgage advice for new borrowers** — Focusing on supporting existing members and their evolving needs (stated as short-term change)
- New borrowers must use a **mortgage intermediary/broker** for purchases and remortgages
- The society works with many mortgage brokers across England and Wales

**Mortgage approach:**
- Practical, case-by-case assessment
- Lending across England and Wales
- Intermediary-only for new business

**Broker partnership:**
- Limited regulatory introducer relationship with **Knight Financial Services Ltd t/a Mortgage Advice Bureau (MAB)** in Loughborough, Leicestershire
- Contact: 01509 413692
- Also accepts other FCA-registered brokers

**Existing borrowers:**
- Can manage mortgages online via secure login
- Full support continues for existing mortgage members

## Community & Values

- **Deep local roots:** Based in Earl Shilton since 1857 — over 167 years of continuous operation in the same village
- **Gilbert family legacy:** The local Gilbert family was associated with running the Society for over 100 years until Mr. John Gilbert retired in the 1990s
- **"as individual as you" ethos:** Every person treated as an individual; personalised products and services
- **Corporate partnership with Action Homeless** — Leicestershire charity and social enterprise committed to improving lives of people affected by homelessness
- **Quarterly token collection scheme** — Members nominate organisations that matter to them and choose how community funding budget is spent; underlines commitment to member engagement
- **Main objectives:** Promote thrift and home ownership — as important today as 160+ years ago
- **Modern environment:** Head office refurbished in 2013 to provide secure, clean, professional and modern environment, demonstrating commitment to Earl Shilton

## Strengths

- Extremely personal service — 40 staff serving ~13,000 members
- Deep community roots in Earl Shilton and Barwell — over 167 years
- Strong member engagement through token collection scheme
- Simple, straightforward product range — easy to understand
- Excellent financial health (as stated by the society)
- Action Homeless partnership demonstrates genuine community commitment
- Friendly, knowledgeable staff — consistently emphasised
- Over 160 years of promoting thrift and home ownership

## Weaknesses

- **Second-smallest** building society in the UK — very limited scale and resources
- **Paused direct mortgage advice** for new borrowers — significant limitation
- Very limited digital capabilities — no app, passbook-based, no online savings opening
- Only 2 branches in a small geographic area
- Only 40 staff — significant capacity constraints and key person risk
- Limited product range compared to larger competitors
- Intermediary-only for new mortgages adds friction and cost for borrowers
- May struggle to attract younger members given traditional/passbook-centric approach
- Extremely localised — limited appeal outside Leicestershire

## Member Demographics

- **Primary members:** Residents of Earl Shilton, Barwell, and surrounding Leicestershire villages
- **Older savers:** Heritage Account (over-50s) and passbook-based model suggest older demographic
- **Younger savers targeted:** Progress Account (under-50s), Young Saver, Junior ISA
- **Mortgage borrowers:** Across England and Wales (via intermediaries), but likely concentrated locally
- **Community-minded individuals:** Token collection scheme appeals to those who want their savings to support local causes
- **Very local focus:** Most members likely within easy reach of Earl Shilton or Barwell branches
