# Hinckley & Rugby Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Hinckley and Rugby Building Society
- **Founded:** 1983 (merger of Hinckley Permanent Building Society, est. 1865, and Rugby Provident Building Society, est. 1861)
- **Origins:** Dates back to 1861 (Rugby Provident) and 1865 (Hinckley Freehold Land and Permanent Building Society)
- **Headquarters:** Upper Bond Street, Hinckley, Leicestershire (moved to new state-of-the-art head office in June 2019, converted from the former Hinckley Magistrates Court)
- **Total assets:** Over £823 million (2025)
- **Savers:** Over 50,000
- **CEO:** Colin Fyfe (appointed November 2018; previously Chief Executive of Darlington Building Society)
- **COO:** Barry Carter
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its members; profits reinvested into the business
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** Straightforward savings and flexible mortgages designed around real people
- **Identity:** A top-20 UK building society with deep roots in Leicestershire and Warwickshire, known for personal service, community commitment, and a family-friendly workplace culture
- **Listed building:** Original Hinckley premises at 37 Castle Street is a Grade II listed building dating back to the 18th century

## Branch Network

Hinckley & Rugby operates **7 branches, 2 agencies, and 1 dedicated Member Services branch** across the East and West Midlands:

The Society is committed to maintaining presence not just in towns and cities but also in smaller village communities in Leicestershire and Warwickshire. Branches and agencies span the two counties.

Key locations include:
- **Hinckley** — Principal office/head office at Upper Bond Street (converted former Magistrates Court)
- Branches and agencies across **Leicestershire and Warwickshire**

## Products

### Savings

Hinckley & Rugby offers a comprehensive range of personal and business savings:

**Personal Savings:**
- **Easy Access** — Flexible deposits and withdrawals
- **Children's Accounts** — Savings for under-18s
- **Cash ISAs** — Tax-free savings
- **Notice Accounts** — Higher rates with notice period
- **Regular Saver Accounts** — For consistent monthly saving
- **Savings Bonds** — Fixed rate for a set term
- **Exclusive Accounts** — Special products for members

**Deposit/Business Accounts:**
- **Business Accounts** — For businesses and organisations
- **Charity Accounts** — Tailored for charities
- **Local Council Accounts** — For local government bodies

**Workplace Savings** — Offered as an employer benefit scheme

### Mortgages

Hinckley & Rugby offers residential mortgages across England and Wales:

**Mortgage types:**
- **New Mortgages** — For first-time buyers, home movers, and remortgagers
- **Green Mortgages** — Products supporting energy-efficient homes
- **Switching Mortgage Deal** — Existing customers switching to new products
- **Borrowing More** — Additional borrowing on existing mortgages

**Key features:**
- Personal mortgage advice
- Monthly repayment calculator and overpayment calculator
- Mortgage Charter compliance
- Options for extending mortgage term or switching to interest-only
- Available through direct and intermediary channels
- Financial support team for members struggling with payments

## Key Differentiators

1. **Top-20 society with local focus** — Over £823 million in assets while maintaining community branch presence including in smaller villages
2. **Green mortgages** — Dedicated green mortgage products supporting energy efficiency
3. **Workplace Savings scheme** — Employer benefit offering (relatively unusual for building societies)
4. **Member Panel** — Active member engagement in Society governance
5. **Financial education** — Schools programme through Leicestershire Cares partnership
6. **Heritage building HQ** — Converted former Magistrates Court provides distinctive character
7. **Experienced CEO** — Colin Fyfe brings experience from Darlington BS and broader financial services
8. **Strong Midlands presence** — Coverage across both Leicestershire and Warwickshire including small villages
9. **New mobile app** — 2025 launch shows ongoing commitment to digital investment

## Strengths

- Strong asset base (£823m+) as a top-20 society
- Good branch and agency network across two counties, including smaller communities
- Modern head office in converted listed building
- Comprehensive savings range including business, charity, and council accounts
- Active community giving (£48,000+ annually)
- New mobile app and digital platform investment
- Family-friendly employer culture

## Weaknesses

- Geographically concentrated in Leicestershire and Warwickshire
- Relatively low profile nationally compared to larger building societies
- Wikipedia data somewhat limited, suggesting lower media/public profile
- Branch and agency numbers have reduced over time (from 12 locations to current 10)
- Competing with both large national lenders and other strong Midlands-based societies

## Member Demographics

- Primarily serves **Leicestershire and Warwickshire** communities
- Over **50,000 savers**
- Mix of personal savers, mortgage borrowers, business depositors, and charity/council account holders
- Family-focused demographic aligned with the Society's emphasis on milestones and financial security
- Local businesses and charities using deposit accounts
- Members who value personal service and local branch access
- Green mortgage customers interested in energy-efficient properties
