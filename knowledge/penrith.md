# Penrith Building Society - Knowledge Base

- **Founded:** 1877
- **Headquarters:** 7 King Street, Penrith, Cumbria CA11 7AR
- **Total assets:** £138 million (December 2022)
- **Members:** 6,965 (December 2022)
- **Staff:** 26 (2022)
- **CEO:** Tim Bowen (Chief Executive)
- **Chair:** Will Lindsay
- **Finance Director:** Elspeth James
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its members, not shareholders
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Area served:** Westmorland and Furness, Cumberland, and wider UK
- **Tagline / positioning:** "It's about more than money — it's about people, community, and growing together"
- **Identity:** Very small, community-focused building society based in the heart of the Lake District; specialist in self-build, expat, holiday let, and buy-to-let mortgages

## Products

### Savings

Penrith Building Society offers savings accounts available to UK residents, operated via branch, post, or electronic transfer.

**Account types:**
- **Instant Access Accounts** — Variable rate savings
- **90 Day Notice ISA** — Tax-free savings with 3.60% interest rate (variable); minimum £100; passbook-operated
- **Notice Accounts** — Higher rates for giving advance notice of withdrawals
- **ISA Accounts** — Cash ISAs (the society only offers Cash ISAs)
- **Other savings products** — Range of competitive accounts

**Key savings features:**
- Passbook-operated accounts
- Can be opened in branch or by post (application packs downloadable from website)
- Payments via faster payment, standing order, cash or cheque in branch
- Total savings limit: £250,000 per member
- One account per individual for certain products (no joint accounts on some)
- ISAs are not flexible ISAs

### Mortgages

Penrith Building Society has a **specialist mortgage offering** reflecting its Lake District location and understanding of rural/complex property situations.

**Mortgage types:**
- **Residential Mortgages** — For home movers and first-time buyers
- **Remortgages** — Switching from another provider or raising capital on an owned property
- **Buy to Let** — For experienced and first-time landlords; capital raising permitted on BTL remortgages
- **Expat Mortgages** — For UK nationals working and residing overseas who want to retain/purchase a UK family home; foreign currency income accepted
- **Holiday Home / Holiday Let** — Core specialism given Lake District location; both second homes and holiday lets
- **Self-Build & Renovation** — Years of expertise in helping customers build dream projects
- **Individual Circumstances** — Multi-income streams, complex financial arrangements, credit blips — personal assessment of each case

**Key mortgage features:**
- Tailored approach — understanding that "life isn't always simple or straightforward"
- 140+ years of mortgage experience
- Works with mortgage advisors/intermediaries
- Preferential rates for existing customers
- Mortgage advice provided through subsidiary Cumbria Mortgage Centre Limited (FCA-regulated)

2. **Expat mortgages** — Specialist offering for overseas workers wanting UK property; accepts foreign currency income
3. **Self-build expertise** — Years of experience in helping members build their own homes
4. **Individual case assessment** — "What might seem unusual to others is normal for us" — flexible approach to complex situations
5. **Last financial institution standing** — Committed to maintaining branch presence in Penrith as banks close
6. **Very small, very personal** — 26 staff serving ~7,000 members means everyone knows each other
7. **Holiday let specialism** — Natural fit for Lake District location; decades of experience

## Strengths

- Unique geographic niche — Lake District/Cumbria specialist
- Strong specialist mortgage products (expat, holiday let, self-build)
- Committed to physical branch presence when others are leaving
- Very personal service — 26 staff for ~7,000 members
- Understanding of rural and complex property situations
- Individual case assessment approach
- Over 145 years of continuous operation

## Weaknesses

- Very small society — £138 million in assets limits capacity and product range
- Single branch — no physical presence outside Penrith
- Limited digital capabilities — no mobile app, passbook-based savings, no online account opening
- Only 26 staff — capacity constraints; succession risk
- Small membership base (~7,000) limits growth potential
- Savings products operated by post/branch — may deter younger savers
- Mortgage advice through subsidiary adds complexity

## Member Demographics

- **Primary members:** Local residents of Penrith, Cumbria, and the wider Lake District area
- **Holiday home/let borrowers:** Investors and second home buyers in the Lake District region
- **Self-builders:** People building or renovating homes in Cumbria and beyond
- **Expats:** UK nationals living overseas who want to maintain/purchase property in the UK
- **Buy-to-let investors:** Landlords in Cumbria and northern England
- **Local savers:** Predominantly local residents who value branch access and personal service
- **Older demographic likely:** Passbook-based savings and single branch suggest older member base
