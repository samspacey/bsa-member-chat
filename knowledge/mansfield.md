# Mansfield Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** The Mansfield Building Society
- **Founded:** 1870
- **Headquarters:** Regent House, Regent Street, Mansfield, Nottinghamshire, NG18 1SS
- **Total assets:** Over £250 million (2012 figure; likely grown since)
- **Staff:** 90+
- **CEO:** Paul Wheeler
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its members; profits reinvested for members' benefit
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** A safe and trusted independent mutual organisation helping customers save and buy homes
- **Identity:** A small, community-rooted building society serving Nottinghamshire and Derbyshire, known for personal service, clear products, and strong community support through its charitable trust and community support scheme
- **Website:** www.mansfieldbs.co.uk

## Digital & Technology

- **Core banking overhaul with TCS** — In September 2024, Mansfield entered into a contract with Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) to replace its core banking software. This is a significant investment for a society of this size and signals serious commitment to modernisation.
- **Online presence** — Website with product information and branch details
- **Intermediary website** — Dedicated portal for authorised intermediaries

## Community & Values

Mansfield Building Society has a structured approach to community giving:

### Charitable Trust
- **Mansfield Building Society Charitable Trust** — Funded through Community Savers accounts
- Supports charities in **Nottinghamshire and Derbyshire**
- Formal grant-making mechanism for community causes

### Community Support Scheme
- Meets **twice per year**
- Typically makes donations of **£100-£1,000** to local community groups
- Accessible to small local organisations

### Volunteering
- Staff are given up to **2 days paid leave per year** to volunteer with local charities and community groups

### Fundraising
- Active staff fundraising for community causes

## Key Differentiators

1. **TCS core banking overhaul** — Major technology investment with Tata Consultancy Services shows forward-looking approach for a small society
2. **Structured community giving** — Charitable Trust plus Community Support Scheme provides two tiers of community funding
3. **Staff volunteering** — 2 days paid volunteering leave per year
4. **Nottinghamshire/Derbyshire focus** — Strong presence in the Mansfield/Ashfield area
5. **Clear, transparent products** — Positioning around straightforward, easy-to-understand savings and mortgages
6. **150+ year heritage** — Serving the community since 1870
7. **Community Savers accounts** — Unique link between member savings and charitable giving

## Strengths

- Major technology investment (TCS core banking) shows commitment to modernisation
- Strong, structured community giving through multiple channels
- Good branch presence across Mansfield/Ashfield area with Saturday opening
- Broader product range than many small societies (insurance, financial advice)
- 150+ years of local heritage and trust
- Staff volunteering culture
- SIPP-eligible accounts broadening market reach

## Weaknesses

- Small asset base (over £250m) — one of the smaller building societies
- Only 4 branches, geographically concentrated in north Nottinghamshire/Derbyshire
- Limited opening hours at some branches (Kirkby closes at 1pm, Chesterfield at 1:30pm)
- Late Wednesday opening (10:00am) across all branches
- Core banking transition (TCS) carries implementation risk
- Low national profile
- Limited digital self-service capabilities (pre-TCS implementation)

## Member Demographics

- Primarily serves **north Nottinghamshire and Derbyshire** communities, particularly Mansfield, Ashfield, and Chesterfield
- Local savers who value branch access and personal service
- Community-minded members — Community Savers directly fund the Charitable Trust
- First-time buyers and home movers in the local area
- Likely includes members who have banked with the Society for generations given 150+ year local presence
- Small businesses and SIPP holders
