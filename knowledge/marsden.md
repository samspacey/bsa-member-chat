# Marsden Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Marsden Building Society
- **Former name:** Great and Little Marsden Permanent Benefit Building Society (1860)
- **Founded:** 29 February 1860, with initial subscriptions of £33 12s 6d
- **Headquarters:** Principal Office, Nelson, Lancashire (built on the site of the former Majestic Theatre, purchased 1961, opened 1964)
- **Total assets:** £500+ million (exceeded £500m for first time in 2019; continued growth since)
- **Members:** ~43,794 (2024)
- **CEO:** Rob Pheasey (appointed 2011 after a long career with the Marsden)
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its saving and borrowing members
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** The largest building society based in Lancashire
- **Identity:** Lancashire's largest independent building society; community-rooted mutual with strong heritage; award-winning customer service

### Mortgages

Marsden Building Society has a distinctive approach to mortgage distribution:

- **New mortgage customers** are directed to **Mortgage Advice Bureau (MAB)** — an independent mortgage broker with access to thousands of products from 90+ lenders
- **Existing mortgage customers** can apply for rate switches and mortgage changes directly with the Marsden
- **Marsden does not offer mortgage advice to new customers directly**
- **Guernsey mortgages** — Introduced in 2018 through partnership with SPF Private Clients

**Key features:**
- Mortgage Advice Bureau acts as intermediary for new business
- MAB fee up to 1% (typical 0.3% of amount borrowed)
- Existing customers have dedicated team for support
- Rate switch service for existing borrowers when product ends
- Guernsey lending through specialist partnership
