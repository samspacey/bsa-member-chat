# Progressive Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Progressive Building Society
- **Founded:** 1914 (from merger of 5 smaller societies in Belfast)
- **Headquarters:** Belfast, Northern Ireland
- **Total assets:** £2 billion+ (milestone reached 2024; £1,900.2 million at December 2022)
- **Revenue:** £28.6 million (2022)
- **Net income:** £7.9 million (2022)
- **Total equity:** £149.5 million (2022)
- **Staff:** Almost 200 (~172 in 2022)
- **CEO:** Michael Boyd (Chief Executive)
- **Chair:** Keith Jess
- **Mutual status:** Owned by and run for the benefit of members; no external shareholders
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA regulated:** Authorised by the Prudential Regulation Authority and regulated by the FCA and PRA (Financial Services Register Number 161841)
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** "Passionate about helping local people to save and champion homeownership"
- **Identity:** Northern Ireland's largest locally owned financial institution; the dominant building society in NI
- **Jurisdiction:** Northern Irish law applies; non-exclusive jurisdiction of Northern Irish Courts

## Branch Network

Progressive Building Society operates **11 branches** across Northern Ireland:

The branches are distributed throughout the province, providing coverage across Northern Ireland's major towns and cities. Belfast serves as the headquarters location.

The society has been undertaking a **branch refurbishment rollout** as part of broader investment in the business.

**Key features:**
- Face-to-face mortgage advice available in branches
- Mortgage appointments available by telephone, video, or in-branch
- Pop-in consultations welcomed
- Savings accounts available across all branches

## Products

### Savings

Progressive offers a comprehensive range of savings products, available both online and in-branch, for the Northern Ireland market.

**Online/Easy Access:**
- **Online Instant Access Saver Account** — Instant access, interest paid annually, from £1
- **Branch Instant Access Saver Account** — In-branch instant access
- **Investment Share Account** — Instant access
- **Progressive Saver Account** — Restricted to four withdrawals per calendar year

**Regular Savers:**
- **Online Regular Saver Account** — Instant access, from £1
- **Clockwork Regular Saver** — One withdrawal per calendar year
- **Online First Home Saver Account** — For Northern Ireland property purchases; instant access
- **First Home Saver** — Branch-based version

**Fixed Rate Bonds (Online & Branch):**
- 1 Year Fixed Rate Access Bond (one withdrawal up to 20% of balance)
- 1 Year Fixed Rate Bond (no withdrawals)
- 2 Year Fixed Rate Bond
- 2 Year Fixed Rate Monthly Income Bond (minimum £25,000)
- 3 Year Fixed Rate Bond
- 5 Year Fixed Rate Bond
- 1 Year Variable Rate Bond
- 2 Year Variable Rate Bond

**Cash ISAs:**
- Cash ISA (instant access, 2.50% variable)
- 1 Year Double Access Cash ISA
- 1 Year Fixed Rate ISA Bond
- 2 Year Fixed Rate ISA Bond
- 3 Year Fixed Rate ISA Bond
- 5 Year Fixed Rate ISA Bond

**Children's/Young People's:**
- Children's and young people's savings options available

**Business/Organisation:**
- Business savings accounts available

### Mortgages

Progressive Building Society **only lends on properties within Northern Ireland** — a key limitation and focus of the business.

**Mortgage options:**
- Residential mortgages (purchase and remortgage)
- First-time buyer products
- Range of fixed and variable rate deals
- Mortgage calculator available online for illustrative repayments

**First-time buyer schemes:**
- **First Home £500 Cashback** — £500 mortgage cashback for First Home Saver account holders who take their first mortgage with Progressive
- **Help to Buy ISA £750 Cashback** — £750 cashback for Help to Buy ISA holders taking their first mortgage with Progressive
- **Member Mortgage Referral Scheme** — Benefits for existing members who refer new mortgage customers

**Mortgage advice:**
- Qualified Mortgage Advisors available for guidance
- Appointments by telephone, video, or face-to-face in branch
- Book online or call 0800 0294 997

## Strengths

- Dominant market position in Northern Ireland
- Strong brand recognition and trust in NI
- Extensive branch network (11 branches) across the province
- Significant digital investment and transformation programme
- Strong financial performance — £2 billion assets milestone
- Comprehensive savings product range (online and branch)
- First Home Saver/mortgage cashback incentives link savings to homeownership
- Nearly 200 staff — well-resourced for a regional society

## Weaknesses

- **Northern Ireland only** for mortgage lending — cannot grow outside NI
- Limited to the NI property market — exposed to local economic conditions
- No presence in Great Britain — savings are NI-focused
- Relatively small by UK-wide standards despite being dominant in NI
- Limited public profile outside Northern Ireland
- Branch refurbishment programme is ongoing — some branches may be dated
- Online product range duplicates branch products, potentially adding complexity

## Member Demographics

- **Primary members:** Northern Ireland residents — both savers and homebuyers
- **First-time buyers:** Key target segment with dedicated products and cashback incentives
- **Northern Irish homeowners:** Mortgage customers across the province
- **Local businesses:** Business savings customers
- **Geographic concentration:** Entirely Northern Ireland-focused
- **All demographics:** Wide appeal across NI society given dominant market position and branch coverage
