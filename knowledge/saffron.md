# Saffron Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Saffron Building Society (formerly Saffron Walden Herts & Essex Building Society; before that Saffron Walden & Essex Building Society; originally Saffron Walden Second Building Society)
- **Founded:** January 1849
- **Founder:** Revd John Marten (Minister of Hill Street Baptist Church and Manager of the Gas Company)
- **First Chairman:** Joseph Durrant (a local builder)
- **Headquarters:** Saffron House, 1a Market Street, Saffron Walden, Essex, CB10 1HX (purpose-built, opened January 1984; officially opened by the Duke of Norfolk, President of the BSA)
- **Total assets:** ~£1,440 million / £1.44 billion (2024)
- **Staff:** ~181
- **CEO:** Colin Field
- **Chairman:** Nick Treble
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its members; regional mutual building society
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes (Registration Number 100015)
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** A regional building society supporting communities across Essex, Hertfordshire, and Suffolk
- **Identity:** A well-established regional building society with 175+ years of heritage, formed through a series of mergers that consolidated its position across the East of England. Known for community involvement, financial wellbeing reviews, and a comprehensive branch network in market towns across Essex, Hertfordshire, and Suffolk.
- **Website:** www.saffronbs.co.uk

## Branch Network

Saffron Building Society operates **8 branches** across Essex, Hertfordshire, and Suffolk (reduced from 11 in 2019 after closing Sawbridgeworth, Great Dunmow, and Frinton):

1. **Saffron Walden** — Market Street, Saffron Walden (head office location, original heartland)
2. **Bishop's Stortford** — Acquired through 1989 merger with Herts & Essex Building Society
3. **Brentwood** — Essex branch
4. **Colchester** — Essex branch
5. **Halstead** — Essex branch
6. **Haverhill** — Suffolk branch (first branch outside Saffron Walden, opened 1969; closed for community volunteering day on 18 Feb 2026)
7. **Royston** — Hertfordshire branch (heritage from 1972 Royston & District BS merger)
8. **Ware** — Hertfordshire branch

## History of Mergers

Saffron Building Society was formed through a series of mergers that built its regional footprint:

1. **1849:** Founded as Saffron Walden Second Building Society
2. **1857:** Became permanent society
3. **1968:** Acquired Saffron Walden & Essex Mechanics Permanent Building Society
4. **1969:** Opened first branch outside Saffron Walden (Haverhill)
5. **1972:** Merged with Royston & District Building Society → became Saffron Walden & District BS
6. **1979:** Merged with London & Essex Building Society (previously North Bow & Manor Park) → became Saffron Walden & Essex BS
7. **1984:** Moved to purpose-built Saffron House, opened by Duke of Norfolk
8. **1989:** Merged with Herts & Essex Building Society (Bishop's Stortford) → became Saffron Walden, Herts & Essex BS
9. **2006:** Changed name to Saffron Building Society
10. **2011:** Assets exceeded £1 billion for the first time
11. **2019:** Closed three branches (Sawbridgeworth, Great Dunmow, Frinton), reducing to 8 branches
