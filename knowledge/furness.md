# Furness Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Furness Building Society
- **Founded:** 28 October 1865
- **Headquarters:** Emlyn Hughes House, Abbey Road, Barrow-in-Furness, Cumbria, LA14 5PQ
- **Total assets:** £1.236 billion (as at 2022, up from £813m in 2010)
- **Members:** ~101,500 (2024)
- **Staff:** ~182 employees
- **CEO:** Simon Broadley
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its saving and borrowing members
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Subsidiaries:** Furness Mortgage Services Ltd, Furness Independent Financial Advisers
- **Tagline / positioning:** "Always with your interest at heart" — community-focused mutual serving the North West and Cumbria
- **Ranking:** Approximately 18th largest UK building society by total assets

## Products

### Savings

Furness offers a comprehensive range of personal savings accounts:

- **Regular Savers** — Monthly payment accounts to build savings over time
- **Everyday Savings (Instant Access)** — Flexible deposit and withdrawal at any time
- **Fixed Rate Bonds** — Lump sum savings at fixed interest rates for set terms
- **Fixed Rate ISAs** — Tax-free savings over a fixed term
- **Cash ISAs** — Flexible, tax-free savings with easy access
- **Notice Accounts** — Higher rates in exchange for giving notice before withdrawals
- **Young Savers** — Accounts designed to help children save for their future
- **Community Accounts** — Save and support a local cause simultaneously

### Mortgages

Furness offers a wide range of mortgage products with individual underwriting:

- **Moving Home** — Standard residential mortgages for home movers
- **Remortgaging** — Switch existing mortgage to potentially save money
- **First Time Buyers** — Tailored products for first-time purchasers
- **Self-Employed** — Flexible approach to assessing self-employed income
- **Buy-to-Let** — For new and experienced landlords
- **Holiday-Let** — Specialist lending for holiday rental properties
- **Lending into Retirement** — Later-life mortgage solutions
- **Short-term Lending / Bridging** — Bridge the gap between mortgage transactions

Key differentiator: Individual case assessment — "we dig deeper to assess each customer's individual circumstances before making a decision."

### Other Products

- **Investments** — Via Furness Independent Financial Advisers subsidiary
- **Loans** — Personal lending products
- **Credit Cards** — Available to members
- **Insurance** — Various insurance products through the IFA arm

## Digital Capabilities

- **Online savings platform** — Partnership with Backbase to develop new digital savings platform
- **Online banking** — Account management and transactions
- **Phone banking** — Expanded call centre (since 2009) for investments and insurance queries
- **No mobile app mentioned** — Digital transformation appears to be ongoing via Backbase partnership

## Community & Values

- Over 160 years of serving the North West and Cumbria
- Named after Emlyn Hughes (legendary footballer from Barrow) for head office
- Community Accounts allow savers to support local causes
- Strong emphasis on personal, face-to-face service
- Member democracy — delegates elected by members to shape the organisation
- Positioned as a genuine community institution for "local people"

## Strengths

- Strong regional presence across Cumbria and Lancashire with 9 branches
- Individual mortgage underwriting (good for complex cases)
- Specialist products: holiday-let, self-employed, bridging, later-life
- Growing society — assets nearly doubled from £813m (2010) to £1.2bn (2022)
- Over 100,000 members — significant community reach for a regional society
- IFA subsidiary provides holistic financial advice

## Weaknesses

- Very regional focus — branches only in Cumbria/Lancashire
- Limited digital presence compared to larger societies
- Digital transformation still in progress (Backbase partnership)
- Small branch footprint relative to asset size
- Limited online profile — relatively few customer reviews

## Member Demographics

- Primarily residents of Cumbria and North Lancashire
- Mix of savers (including young savers) and mortgage borrowers
- Self-employed and complex-case borrowers attracted by individual underwriting
- Holiday-let investors (Lake District area is prime holiday rental territory)
- Retirees and later-life borrowers
- Community-minded savers using Community Accounts
