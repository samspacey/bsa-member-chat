# Newbury Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Newbury Building Society (originally The Newbury Permanent Benefit Building and Investment Society)
- **Founded:** 1856
- **Headquarters:** 90 Bartholomew Street, Newbury, West Berkshire, RG14 5EE
- **Total assets:** £1.54 billion (October 2023); crossed the £1 billion mark in 2017
- **Members:** Over 76,000
- **Staff:** 180-190
- **CEO:** Phillippa Cardno (appointed 2022; joined the Society in 1996, celebrating 30 years in 2026)
- **Previous CEO:** Roland Gardner (2007-2022; joined in 2001)
- **Mutual status:** Owned by members; profits reinvested for members' benefit
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA/PRA regulated:** Yes (Registration Number 206077)
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** "Something different. Something better. Something mutual."
- **Identity:** A proudly independent mutual building society with 165+ years of heritage, serving southern England through a 10-branch network across Berkshire, Hampshire, and Oxfordshire. Known for excellent customer service, community involvement, and financial education.
- **Awards:** Won Best Building Society at the 2021 British Bank Awards; Won Trusted Quality Provider at the 2025 UK Customer Satisfaction Awards (beating John Lewis and BT)
- **Website:** www.newbury.co.uk

## Products

### Savings

Newbury offers a full range of savings products:

**Account types:**
- **Easy Access** — Flexible deposits and withdrawals
- **ISAs** — Tax-free Cash ISAs
- **Restricted Access** — Higher rates with limits on withdrawals
- **Children's Savings** — Accounts for young savers
- **Fixed Rate Bonds** — Guaranteed rates for fixed terms
- **Business Savings** — For businesses and organisations

**Key features:**
- Online account opening for new and existing customers
- **myaccounts** — Online savings management platform
- Savings calculator tool
- Book savings appointments online
- Confirmation of Payee supported
- FSCS information readily available
- Savings promises published

- CEO succession may need planning given long-serving leadership
- Limited national brand recognition outside its core area
