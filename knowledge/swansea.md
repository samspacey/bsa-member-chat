# Swansea Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Swansea Building Society
- **Founded:** 6 February 1923 (by local estate agent John Oliver Watkins)
- **Headquarters:** 11 Cradock Street, Swansea, SA1 3EW, Wales
- **Total assets:** £607.0 million (December 2023, up 14.5% on 2022)
- **Net income:** £6.2 million (December 2023, up 14.9% on 2022)
- **Staff:** ~80
- **CEO:** Alun Williams (Chief Executive)
- **Chair:** Stephen Maddock (Non-Executive Chairman)
- **Director of Finance:** Nathan Griffiths
- **Director of Risk and Compliance:** Catherine Griffiths
- **Mutual status:** Independent mutual building society, owned by its members
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** Wales-based mutual providing personal, friendly service
- **Identity:** Independent Welsh building society with strong local roots in Swansea; bilingual (Welsh/English) service
- **Website available in Welsh (Cymraeg)**

## Branch Network

Swansea Building Society operates branches in the Swansea area:

1. **Head Office / Main Branch** — 11 Cradock Street, Swansea, SA1 3EW (Tel: 01792 739100)
   - Open Monday-Friday 9am-4:45pm (closed weekends at present)
2. **Additional branches** — The society expanded outside Swansea city centre for the first time during/after the 2008-2009 Icelandic financial crisis, when local savers sought safer institutions

*Note: The society historically operated primarily from its Swansea city centre location, with limited expansion.*

## Products

### Savings

Swansea Building Society offers a range of personal and business savings accounts with competitive rates.

**Personal savings accounts:**
- **Instant Access Saver** — Variable rate (3.00% from Feb 2026), minimum £1
- **Regular Monthly Saver** — 3.75% gross / 3.82% AER, deposits £25-£2,500 per calendar month
- **90 Day Notice Account** — 3.50% gross / 3.56% AER, minimum £1
- **Cash ISA Instant Access** — 3.00% tax-free, minimum £1
- **Cash ISA 90 Day Notice** — 3.50% tax-free, minimum £1
- **First Adult Saver Account** — For ages 18-40, 3.25% gross / 3.29% AER
- **Young Saver** — 3.75% gross / 3.79% AER
- **Junior ISA** — 3.75% tax-free

**Business savings accounts:**
- **Business Instant Access Saver** — 2.50%, minimum £1
- **Business 90 Day Notice** — 3.00% gross / 3.04% AER, minimum £1

**Historical/closed accounts:**
- Cygnet / Swans Junior Account — 3.75% (linked to Swansea City AFC partnership from 2010)

### Mortgages

Swansea Building Society provides mortgage products for the Welsh market and beyond.

**Mortgage types:**
- Residential mortgages (purchase and remortgage)
- Range of fixed and variable rate products
- Personal mortgage advice from dedicated Mortgage Managers

**Application process:**
- Available directly through the society
- Mortgage literature and forms available online
- Dedicated mortgage sales team with named managers
- Intermediary service also available

## Community & Values

- **Welsh identity:** Proudly Welsh, with bilingual service and deep roots in Swansea since 1923
- **Founded by local businessman:** John Oliver Watkins, a local estate agent, established the society
- **Swansea City AFC partnership:** In 2010, launched a savings account in partnership with Swansea City Football Club, earning the club additional revenue through commission
- **Personal service:** Emphasises warm, friendly personal service as a key differentiator from larger financial organisations
- **Icelandic crisis beneficiary:** The society gained savers during the 2008-2009 Icelandic banking crisis as people sought safer local institutions
- **Community-focused:** Strong emphasis on serving the local Swansea and wider Welsh community

## Key Differentiators

1. **Welsh identity** — One of only a few building societies based in Wales; bilingual service
2. **Independent mutual** — Not part of a larger group; maintains full independence
3. **Personal, friendly service** — Small enough to offer genuine personal attention
4. **Strong growth** — 14.5% asset growth in 2023 shows momentum
5. **Young saver focus** — First Adult Saver (18-40) and Junior ISA/Young Saver products
6. **Local roots** — Over 100 years serving the Swansea community
7. **Digital transition** — Actively modernising with new MyAccounts platform

## Strengths

- Strong financial performance with consistent growth
- Deep local roots in Swansea with over 100 years of history
- Bilingual Welsh/English service — unique cultural positioning
- Personal, friendly service emphasised throughout the organisation
- Good range of savings products with competitive rates
- Young saver and first-time buyer focus
- Independent mutual status

## Weaknesses

- Small branch network — limited physical presence
- Closed on weekends — may not suit all members
- Limited online platform (transitioning from Swansea Online to MyAccounts)
- Small staff team (~80) means capacity constraints
- Geographic concentration in Swansea area
- Limited mortgage product details publicly available
- Website can be difficult to navigate (bilingual structure)

## Member Demographics

- **Primary members:** Savers and borrowers in the Swansea area and wider South Wales
- **Young savers:** Targeted through First Adult Saver (18-40) and children's/junior accounts
- **Welsh speakers:** Bilingual service attracts Welsh-speaking members
- **Local businesses:** Business savings accounts for Swansea-area organisations
- **Football fans:** Historical Swansea City AFC partnership attracted local supporters
- **Safety-conscious savers:** Attracted members during the Icelandic crisis who valued local, safe institutions
