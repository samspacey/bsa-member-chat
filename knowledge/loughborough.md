# Loughborough Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** The Loughborough Building Society
- **Founded:** 1867 (as the Loughborough Permanent Investment, Land and Building Society)
- **Headquarters:** Loughborough, Leicestershire
- **Total assets:** ~£533 million (2023)
- **Staff:** ~85 (2023), ~74 (2025 estimate)
- **CEO:** Gary Brebner
- **Mutual status:** Owned by its members
- **BSA member:** Yes
- **FCA regulated:** Yes
- **FSCS protected:** Yes
- **Tagline / positioning:** A local, community-focused building society serving Leicestershire, Derbyshire, and Nottinghamshire
- **Identity:** A small-to-medium building society with deep local roots in the East Midlands, offering personal service through a small branch network. Known as "The Loughborough" by locals.
- **Website:** www.theloughborough.co.uk

## Branch Network

Loughborough Building Society operates **4 branches** plus **1 agency**:

1. **Loughborough** — Main branch and head office location
2. **Derby** — Branch serving the Derbyshire market
3. **Long Eaton** — Branch in the south Derbyshire/Nottinghamshire border town
4. **Anstey** — Branch in the Leicestershire village
5. **Southwell** — Agency in the Nottinghamshire market town

This compact network covers parts of Leicestershire, Derbyshire, and Nottinghamshire.

## Products

### Savings

The Loughborough offers savings products for personal savers:

**Account types (typical for a society of this size):**
- Easy Access savings
- ISAs (Cash ISAs)
- Fixed Rate Bonds
- Notice Accounts
- Regular Saver Accounts
- Children's Accounts

### Mortgages

The Society provides residential mortgage products:

**Mortgage types:**
- Standard residential mortgages
- First-time buyer mortgages
- Remortgages
- Moving home mortgages
- Available both directly and through intermediaries

## Digital & Technology

- **Online presence** — Website with account information and product details
- **LMS Panel Link** — Joined in September 2024, connecting the Society with law firms for mortgage processing
- **Branch-focused model** — The small branch network suggests a more traditional, personal-service approach

## Community & Values

- **Mutual ethos:** Member-owned with profits reinvested for members' benefit
- **Local community focus:** Branches in smaller East Midlands communities like Anstey and Long Eaton, plus the market town of Southwell
- **Long heritage:** Over 155 years serving the local community since 1867

## Key Differentiators

1. **Hyper-local focus** — Small branch network serving specific East Midlands communities
2. **Personal service** — Small staff (~74-85) enables genuine personal relationships
3. **155+ year heritage** — Deep roots in the Loughborough area since 1867
4. **Small community presence** — Branches in villages and market towns (Anstey, Southwell) rather than just cities
5. **Mutual independence** — Long-standing independent mutual serving local members

## Strengths

- Strong local identity in the East Midlands
- Personal, face-to-face service through intimate branch network
- Over 155 years of heritage and community trust
- LMS Panel Link joining (2024) shows modernisation of mortgage processing
- Presence in both urban (Derby) and smaller community settings

## Weaknesses

- Small asset base (~£533m) limits competitive positioning on rates
- Very small branch network (4 branches + 1 agency)
- Small staff limits capacity for product innovation and marketing
- Limited digital capabilities compared to larger societies
- Low national profile — primarily known locally
- Limited product range compared to larger competitors

## Member Demographics

- Primarily serves **Leicestershire, Derbyshire, and Nottinghamshire** residents
- Local savers who value branch access and personal service
- First-time buyers and home movers in the East Midlands
- Community-minded members with ties to Loughborough and surrounding areas
- Likely skews towards older, more traditional banking customers who prefer face-to-face service
