# Chelsea Building Society - Knowledge Base

## Overview

- **Full legal name:** Chelsea Building Society — a trading name of Yorkshire Building Society
- **Founded:** 1875 (as London & Camberwell Building Society); Chelsea and South London Building Society formed 1966 by merger; renamed Chelsea Building Society 1971
- **Historical headquarters:** Thirlestaine Hall, Cheltenham, Gloucestershire (since 1973)
- **Current headquarters:** Yorkshire House, Yorkshire Drive, Bradford BD5 8LJ (YBS Group)
- **Status:** **No longer an independent society.** Merged with Yorkshire Building Society in 2010. Chelsea was the fourth largest building society in the UK at the time of merger, with assets exceeding £13 billion and 35 branches.
- **Current operation:** Online-only savings and mortgage brand under YBS Group
- **BSA member:** Yes (through Yorkshire Building Society; Registration number 106085)
- **FCA regulated:** Yes (through YBS)
- **FSCS protected:** Yes

## History

- **1875:** London & Camberwell Building Society established
- **1878:** Borough of Chelsea Permanent Building Society formed
- **1966:** The two societies merged to form Chelsea and South London Building Society
- **1971:** Renamed Chelsea Building Society
- **1973:** Administrative headquarters moved from Streatham to Thirlestaine Hall, Cheltenham
- **1988:** Merged with City of London Building Society
- **2005:** Opened purpose-built customer contact centre near head office with award-winning environmental credentials (geothermal heating, green building)
- **2007:** Acquired Britannia Capital Securities (secured loan and mortgage brokers)
- **2008:** Acquired Catholic Building Society
- **October 2008:** Revealed £55 million of liquid assets (1.55%) invested in troubled Icelandic banks
- **August 2009:** Wrote off £41 million in "potentially fraudulent loans" from Buy to Let mortgage book (2006-2008 vintage)
- **2009:** Reported net loss of £29 million for H1 2009; Moody's downgraded rating to Baa3
- **2010:** Merged with Yorkshire Building Society due to financial difficulties
- **January 2016:** YBS announced seven remaining Chelsea branches would close, with remainder rebranded as Yorkshire
- **Post-2016:** Chelsea became an online-only brand offering basic savings and mortgage products

## Branch Network

- **No branches** — Chelsea Building Society is now an online-only brand
- **Historical:** Had 35 branches at time of merger, mainly in southern England and London
- All former Chelsea branches were either closed or rebranded as Yorkshire Building Society branches by 2016

## Financial Difficulties (2008-2010)

Chelsea's merger with Yorkshire Building Society was precipitated by a series of financial difficulties:

1. **Icelandic bank exposure (2008):** £55 million invested in Icelandic banks during the financial crisis
2. **Fraudulent loans (2009):** £41 million written off from the Buy to Let mortgage book, mainly from loans underwritten 2006-2008
3. **Net loss (2009):** £29 million loss for H1 2009
4. **Credit downgrade:** Moody's downgraded to Baa3, making it harder to attract deposits
5. **Loss of confidence:** Members and rating agencies lost confidence in the society
6. **Merger (2010):** Transfer of engagements to Yorkshire Building Society

3. **Part of YBS Group** — Alongside Yorkshire Building Society and Norwich & Peterborough Building Society

## Strengths (Historical)

- Was the fourth largest building society in the UK
- Strong brand recognition, particularly in southern England
- Award-winning environmental credentials at contact centre
- Long heritage dating back to 1875

## Weaknesses

- No longer independent — brand is a legacy trading name of YBS
- Reputation damaged by Icelandic bank exposure and fraudulent loans
- All branches closed or rebranded — no physical presence
- Very limited current product range (basic online savings and mortgages)
- Brand has limited distinct identity compared to Yorkshire Building Society
- Historical financial difficulties are a matter of public record
